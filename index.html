Week 4 — About the Topic

Who was she?

A Life of Discovery

Early Life

Marie Skłodowska Curie was born on November 7, 1867, in Warsaw, Poland. Growing up under Russian imperial rule, she excelled academically despite restrictions on women's education. She secretly attended the "Floating University" — an underground Polish school — before moving to Paris to pursue her scientific dreams.

Scientific Career

At the University of Paris (Sorbonne), Marie became the first woman to earn a physics degree. Alongside her husband Pierre Curie, she pioneered research on radioactivity — a term she herself coined. Her relentless experimentation in a leaky shed led to the discovery of two new elements: polonium and radium.

Nobel Prizes

Marie Curie is the only person in history to have won Nobel Prizes in two different sciences. She received the Nobel Prize in Physics in 1903 (shared with Pierre and Henri Becquerel) and the Nobel Prize in Chemistry in 1911, making her an icon of scientific achievement and gender equality.

Legacy

Beyond her discoveries, Marie Curie inspired generations of women in science. During World War I, she developed mobile X-ray units — nicknamed "petites Curies" — that helped treat wounded soldiers. She died on July 4, 1934, from aplastic anemia caused by prolonged radiation exposure.

Key Facts

Discoveries & Achievements

⚗️ Scientific Discoveries

  • Coined the term "radioactivity"
  • Discovered the element Polonium (Po)
  • Discovered the element Radium (Ra)
  • Proved atoms emit radiation spontaneously
  • Developed techniques to isolate radioactive isotopes
  • Invented mobile X-ray units (WWI)

🏅 Major Awards & Honours

  1. Nobel Prize in Physics — 1903
  2. Nobel Prize in Chemistry — 1911
  3. Davy Medal — Royal Society, 1903
  4. Matteucci Medal — 1904
  5. Elliott Cresson Medal — 1909
  6. First female professor at Sorbonne

📅 Key Life Events

  • Born: November 7, 1867 — Warsaw, Poland
  • Moved to Paris to study — 1891
  • Married Pierre Curie — 1895
  • Discovered Polonium & Radium — 1898
  • First Nobel Prize — 1903
  • Second Nobel Prize — 1911
  • Founded Curie Institute, Paris — 1920
  • Died: July 4, 1934

🔬 Fields of Study

  1. Physics — radioactivity & atomic theory
  2. Chemistry — element isolation techniques
  3. Mathematics — quantitative analysis
  4. Medicine — radiotherapy applications
  5. Engineering — X-ray machine design
